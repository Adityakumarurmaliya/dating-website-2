    • Safe Online Communication

    • Protect Your Personal Information: Avoid sharing sensitive information like your home address, phone number, or financial details with someone you've just met online. Keep your personal information private until you're confident in the person's trustworthiness.
    • Use the Dating Platform: Stick to the dating website's messaging system for initial conversations. Dating sites often have safety measures in place to monitor and filter messages, helping to prevent scams and harassment.

    Meeting Safely

    • Verify Their Identity: Before meeting, confirm the person's identity. Ask for additional photos, video chat, or even use a background check service if you have any doubts. Scammers often avoid such requests.
    • Meet in a Public Place: When planning your first meeting, choose a public location with plenty of people around, such as a coffee shop, restaurant, or park. Avoid secluded or private areas, especially for the initial meeting

    • No Misrepresentation: Users should provide accurate information in their profiles. This includes using recent and truthful photos, as well as honest descriptions of themselves.
    • No Scams or Spam: Scamming or spamming other users is strictly prohibited. This includes financial requests, phishing attempts, or any fraudulent activities.
